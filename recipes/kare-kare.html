Sizzling Sisig

"Beef kare-kare isn’t just a Filipino dish—it’s a full-on family reunion in a pot. Tender beef swimming in a rich peanut sauce so thick, it could double as a hug on a rainy day. Add in veggies for crunch, bagoong for that salty kick, and suddenly you’ve got the kind of meal that makes everyone fight for the last spoonful of rice. It’s messy, nutty, and oh-so-satisfying—just like the best Filipino feasts should be."

Ingredients:

  • Mantik ung galing sa 'yong mukha
  • Wangba!
  • kilo beef cubes
  • Seboyas sabi ni Jiphre na Manileño
  • 1/4 Cup Atsuete Seeds (annatto seeds) soaked in 1 cup water
  • Water from any river
  • 1/4 Cup peanut butter
  • Puso ng saging
  • Talong na Brandnew
  • Sitaw
  • 1/2 Cup peanuts ground into a paste
  • Petchay ung kasing ganda ng kapitbahay nyo
  • Asin

Step by Step parang sayaw:

  1. Heat oil in a large pot. Sauté garlic and onions until translucent.
  2. Add beef. Strain annatto seeds from the water, discard the seeds, and add the annatto water to the pot. Add water and peanut butter to the p
  3. Add the vegetables: puso ng saging, talong, and sitaw. Simmer for 30 minutes or until vegetables are tender. Remove vegetables from the pot and set aside.
  4. Add ground peanuts. Stir and simmer until the sauce has thickened.
  5. Continue to simmer until the beef cubes are tender. Once the beef cubes are tender, add Petchay. Return the vegetables to the pot and simmer until reheated. Season to taste.
